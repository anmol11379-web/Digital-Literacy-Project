# Cybercrime Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

## What is UPI Fraud?
UPI and online payment fraud is a common cybercrime where attackers trick users into sharing **sensitive financial information** or approving **fraudulent transactions**.

These scams usually happen through:
- Fake phone calls  
- Fraudulent messages  
- Malicious links  
that appear to be from banks, payment apps, or customer support.

##  How the Fraud Happens (Step-by-Step)
1. The attacker contacts the victim claiming an issue or offering a reward/refund  
2. The victim is asked to click a **malicious link** or approve a payment request  
3. The attacker may ask for **OTP or PIN** (which should never be shared)  
4. Once approved/shared, money is instantly transferred to the attacker  

##  Who is Targeted?
-  Students  
-  Elderly people  
-  Users unfamiliar with digital payments  

##  Consequences
-  Financial loss  
-  Stress and anxiety  
-  Loss of trust in digital systems  


##  Key Takeaway
> Always verify requests carefully and **never share confidential information** like OTP or PIN.

✔ Awareness and caution are the best defenses against online payment fraud.
